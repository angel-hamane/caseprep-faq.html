Probate Case Prep – Frequently Asked Questions

This course helps you organize facts, evidence, and strategy inputs so you’re ready for court or a lawyer consult. It is not petition drafting or legal advice.

What is Probate Case Prep? How is it different from your petition course?

Case Prep is pre-petition work: understanding process, identifying legal grounds, and assembling a clean case binder (timeline, documents, witness notes). The flagship petition course focuses on drafting and filing documents. Many students take Case Prep first to save time and money later.

Who is this for?

For people considering probate litigation in California who want to clarify if they have a case and organize evidence before meeting an attorney or filing pro per.

What will I have by the end?

A structured case binder: a dated timeline, organized records (medical/financial where relevant), a list of potential witnesses, and a map of possible legal grounds tied to supporting evidence.

Do you tell me exactly what to file or how to word my petition?

No. This course is educational and focuses on preparation and organization, not drafting filings. For legal advice or document drafting, consult a licensed probate attorney or enroll in the separate petition course when available.

Can you review my documents or tell me if I will win?

No. I can’t review personal materials or predict outcomes. The course helps you evaluate potential grounds and assemble evidence so a lawyer—or the court—can understand your case more quickly.

Will this course cover every county’s procedures?

No. Concepts are California-focused, but local rules vary by county. Case Prep teaches how to organize and think through the case; always check your local court’s rules.

What are “grounds,” and will we work on them here?

Yes—at a preparation level. We examine common grounds (lack of capacity, undue influence, financial elder abuse, fraud/forgery/duress/mistake/improper execution) and the types of evidence that tend to support each, so you can assess fit and gaps.

Will this help me talk to a lawyer more efficiently?

That’s the goal. Walking into a consult with a timeline, key records, and clear questions can shorten paid time significantly and improve the quality of advice.

How long do I have access? Is it self-paced?

Self-paced, on-demand. Access terms will be listed on the checkout page at launch.

What counts as useful evidence for my binder?

Depends on the ground, but examples include dated emails/texts, medical records showing decline, financial statements, sudden changes to estate documents, witness notes, and a clean timeline tying events to allegations.

How do I handle sensitive documents?

Redact private numbers when organizing, keep originals secure, and store copies in clearly labeled folders. Follow any protective orders or privacy rules that apply.

Does this course address deadlines (statute of limitations)?

Yes, at a high level so you understand why timing matters. It does not calculate your deadline. If timing is uncertain, consult a probate attorney immediately.

What are my options after I finish?

Use your binder to (1) consult a probate attorney, (2) continue learning in the petition course when available, or (3) decide not to proceed. The value is clarity before spending more time or money.
