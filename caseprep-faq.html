Probate Case Prep – Frequently Asked Questions

This course helps you prepare for court (or an attorney consult) by organizing facts, evidence, and a clean case binder. It is not petition drafting or legal advice.

About the Course
What is Probate Case Prep? How is it different from the free course?

The free course helps you decide if you’re in the right place (probate vs. probate litigation) and introduces standing and deadlines. Case Prep is the next step: you’ll build a dated timeline, organize evidence, and map potential legal grounds so you’re ready for action.

Who is this course for?

People in California who think they may have a probate litigation case and want clarity and organization before meeting a lawyer or filing pro per.

What will I have by the end?
  • A clean timeline of key events.
  • Organized records (medical, financial, communications) tied to potential grounds.
  • A simple witness list and document index.
  • Questions to take into a lawyer consult—or to continue DIY next steps.
Scope & Limitations
Is this legal advice? Will you review my personal documents?

No. This is an educational course. I don’t review personal files, give legal advice, or predict outcomes. For case-specific guidance, consult a licensed probate attorney.

Does Case Prep guarantee I have a case or that I will win?

No. Case Prep helps you assess potential grounds and organize evidence. Only the court can decide standing, validity, and outcome.

Do you calculate my statute of limitations or draft my petition?

No. We discuss timing at a high level so you understand urgency, but we don’t compute deadlines or draft filings. Petition drafting is covered in the separate flagship course.

Organizing Evidence
What counts as useful evidence for my binder?

Depends on the ground. Examples: dated emails/texts; medical records showing decline; financial statements or account activity; sudden document changes; witness notes with dates and context.

How should I handle sensitive information?

Redact personal numbers on shared copies, keep originals secure, and label folders clearly. Follow any privacy rules or protective orders that apply.

Do you provide checklists or templates?

Yes—simple timeline, binder index, and evidence-mapping worksheets to help you stay organized.

Timing & Next Steps
Will this help with a lawyer consultation?

That’s the goal. A clean timeline and document set can shorten paid consult time and improve the quality of advice.

What should I do after Case Prep?

Use your binder to (1) consult a probate attorney, (2) continue into the petition course when available, or (3) decide not to proceed. The value is clarity before spending more time or money.
